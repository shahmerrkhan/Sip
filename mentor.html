why it matters

your time is the point,
not the problem.

Most platforms treat your calendar like a commodity. Sip treats it like what it is — genuinely valuable.

🎯
intent-first requests only

Every person who reaches out has to say what they want and why. No vague "pick your brain" messages. Just people with something real to figure out.

🔐
on/off when you need it

Toggle your availability with one click. Busy quarter? Close the door. Got 30 minutes free? Open it. No commitments, no guilt.

📬
no cold message noise

Requests hit your email, not your LinkedIn DMs. You read, you decide, you click a link. The whole thing takes under 10 seconds on your end.

🌱
real impact, no theater

One honest conversation can change someone's trajectory. Sip makes it easy to show up for the next generation without turning it into a part-time job.

getting set up

five minutes to listed.

No onboarding calls. No verification queue. Just a clean profile and you're live.

01
create your profile

Name, role, company, and a one-liner on what you're happy to talk about. No essay required.

02
drop your calendar link

Calendly, Google Calendar, whatever you use. Sip doesn't manage your calendar — it just points people to it when you say yes.

03
toggle open

Flip the switch and you're live in the directory. People can now see you and send requests.

04
get an email, click a link

Someone reaches out, you get a clean email with who they are and what they want. Click accept and the calendar link goes to them automatically.
